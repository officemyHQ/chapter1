Before you sell myHQ, you need to know myHQ. This chapter maps the company — where it came from, who built it, how it's organised, and where it's headed. Every deal you pitch, every question you answer, and every relationship you build will be stronger when you know this story cold.

myHQ started with one question asked in Bangalore traffic in 2015. Today it is India's fastest-growing commercial real estate platform — 50,000+ clients, ₹500 Cr+ GMV annually, and a 2030 target of ₹5,000 Cr in transactions. The journey from a friend's café to a national platform is the story you carry into every room.

The Timeline

myHQ's story is a ten-year arc from a single empty café to India's largest flex workspace platform. Knowing this history gives you credibility in every client conversation — because the company's resilience, its decisions under pressure, and its backers all signal the quality of the platform you represent.

2015 · The Spark
Two IIT Delhi batchmates. One question. Why can't people work from wherever they want?
Utkarsh Kawatra (ex-Helion Ventures) and Vinayak Aggarwal (ex-Goldman Sachs) had known each other since IIT Delhi. Years later, stuck in Bangalore traffic together, the same question kept coming up: why can't people work from wherever they actually want? That question is where myHQ came from.
Did you know · Why these two, specifically?
Utkarsh and Vinayak weren't random co-founders. They'd known each other since IIT Delhi — but didn't start anything together back then. Utkarsh went into VC at Helion. Vinayak went to Goldman Sachs as a quant. The idea waited five years until they were both in Bangalore at the same time, frustrated by the same thing. Co-founder fit isn't a luxury — it's a precondition.
The Insight
2016 · The Move
A friend's café sat empty every morning. Empty chairs became the first workspace.
A friend ran a café in Delhi NCR that sat dead every morning — chairs up, tables empty, burning rent before the evening rush. Utkarsh and Vinayak looked at those empty chairs and saw a workspace. That's where myHQ actually starts.
Did you know · What did the founders quit to do this?
Utkarsh walked away from a VC role at Helion Ventures. Vinayak walked away from Goldman Sachs as a Quant & Algorithmic Trading Strategist. Both jobs paid extremely well. They left to run a day-pass concept out of someone else's café. The opportunity cost was very real.
The Origin
Apr 2016 · Founded
Day pass model. Free WiFi. Discounted menus. No deposit, no commitment.
Come in, get WiFi, order at a discounted rate, work. No deposit, no monthly commitment. The café that used to sit empty before noon started filling up with freelancers, remote workers, and early founders — confirming the core insight: idle space is wasted potential.
Did you know · How small was the original team?
Two co-founders. One café. Zero employees. The first year was Utkarsh and Vinayak doing literally everything — supply, demand, customer support, ops. Most early signups came from Utkarsh standing at the café entrance and pitching the concept to walk-ins.
Founded
2016–17 · First Legs
No ads. Just word of mouth. One café became 20+ venues across Delhi NCR.
They didn't run ads. People just told other people. One café became two, then five, then 20+ venues across Delhi NCR — Social, Philips MTV, CCD. The concept had legs; now it needed wings.
2017 · Angel Round
Neeraj Singh (Spinny) · Ashneer Grover (BharatPe)
Did you know · Why founders, not VCs?
The 2017 round wasn't institutional capital. It was founder-to-founder belief. Neeraj Singh was building Spinny. Ashneer Grover was about to start BharatPe. They wrote cheques because they recognised the zero-to-one pattern. VCs were still figuring out if coworking was a real category. Operators saw it first.
20+ Spaces2017 Angel Round
2017–18 · Growing Up
Cafés were the start. Coworking was next. The Day Pass is born.
Cafés could only scale so far. They moved fast: 100+ coworking spaces listed on the platform in short order. And with that came a new product — the Day Pass. Book a day, show up, work, leave. Pay only for what you use.
2018 · Pre-Series A
Anupam Mittal
Did you know · Why did the Day Pass change everything?
Cafés had no infrastructure for work. Coworking spaces solved that. But operators still sold annual memberships. The Day Pass was the unlock: take coworking-grade space and price it like a coffee. Suddenly the addressable market wasn't just full-time freelancers — it was every professional with a laptop and one productive day to spend.
100+ SpacesDay Pass Born
2018–19 · First Products
A Shopify store. Day passes. Coworking listings. Scrappy, but it worked.
The platform at this stage was anything but polished. Virtual office services ran on a Shopify store. Day passes were the core transaction. Coworking listings were being added one by one. None of this was elegant — but it was real. Paying customers. Real inventory. Actual transactions.
2019 · Series A
Led by India Quotient
Did you know · Where was the team actually working from?
Started in Utkarsh's parents' basement, then moved to a 2BHK apartment in Delhi, then to a small office. The team was running a workspace marketplace from rented residential rooms. They used the product themselves before insisting clients did.
India Quotient 2019Day Passes
2020–21 · The Test
COVID shut everything down. Buyout offers came in. They said no to every one.
COVID didn't ease them into a tough patch — it just turned the lights off. Multiple buyout offers came in. The founders said no to every single one. Then ANAROCK came — not as a rescue, but as the right fit: India's largest CRE advisory, and they wanted myHQ to keep being myHQ.
Did you know · Why ANAROCK and not anyone else?
Most buyout offers were fire-sale offers from people who saw a discounted asset. ANAROCK was different. They're India's biggest CRE advisory — they understood real estate at the deepest level, and they wanted myHQ to keep operating as myHQ. Not absorbed. Not rebranded. The founders stayed. The mission stayed.
ResilienceANAROCK 2021
2021–22 · New Chapter
Three products, properly built. Four cities. A real operation.
With ANAROCK's backing, the verticals that had been forming for years were now fully live — and the team finally had the infrastructure to scale them properly. The invisible change was credibility on enterprise calls. Pre-ANAROCK, myHQ was "a coworking app." Post-ANAROCK, it was "myHQ by ANAROCK" — unlocking deal sizes that weren't reachable before.
3 Verticals Live4 Cities
Today · 2026
India's fastest growing CRE platform. And they're just getting started.
From a friend's café with empty chairs to India's fastest-growing CRE platform. Same mission. Completely different scale. myHQ is now taking the model global through myhqspaces.com — same operator-agnostic advisory, new geographies.
TodayGoing Global
2030 · The Ambition
35X in 3 years. 10X more by 2030. ₹5,000 Cr in transactions.
From ₹20 Cr to ₹1,200 Cr. The next chapter ends at ₹5,000 Cr in transactions, ₹500 Cr in revenue, and a global footprint. Same platform. Same model. India's fastest growing CRE company — and the market is just getting started.

Three Verticals

myHQ operates three distinct business lines under one platform. Each vertical serves a different customer need, runs its own PnL, and has its own leadership. They share infrastructure, brand, and operator relationships — but each is a standalone product with its own market.
