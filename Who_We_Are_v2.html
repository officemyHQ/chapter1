This chapter helps you understand the story behind myHQ and maps the company — where it came from, who built it, how it's organized, and where it's headed. It is the story of a dream, a vision, and a journey of building something meaningful from the ground up.

myHQ started with a simple question asked in Bangalore traffic in 2015. Today, it has grown into one of India's fastest-growing commercial real estate platforms, serving 50,000+ clients, facilitating ₹500 Cr+ in annual GMV, and pursuing an ambitious goal of ₹5,000 Cr in transactions by 2030.

The journey from a friend's café to an emerging international platform is more than a company story — it reflects the ambition, resilience, and long-term vision that continue to shape the future of workspaces and commercial real estate.

Learning Outcome: Understand every phase of myHQ's journey — the bets, the turning points, and what shaped the company it is today.
01

The Story

myHQ didn't start with a funding announcement or a grand plan. It started with a question asked in a Delhi NCR café in 2015 — and a friend's empty chairs that no one was using. What followed was ten years of building, breaking, rebuilding, and refusing to quit. This is that story.

2015 · Phase 1 begins
One question. Why can't people work from wherever they want?
Utkarsh Kawatra and Vinayak Aggarwal were IIT Delhi Alumni who'd gone their separate ways — Utkarsh to Helion Ventures, Vinayak to Goldman Sachs. They ended up back in NCR at the same time, frustrated by the same thing: good professionals wasting hours commuting to offices they didn't need to be in. The question wasn't complicated. The answer would take a decade to build.
The Insight
Apr 2016 · Founded
A friend's café. Empty chairs every morning. That became the first supply on the platform.
A friend ran a café that stayed empty before noon — chairs up, tables empty, burning rent before the evening rush. Utkarsh and Vinayak saw something no one else was seeing: idle real estate is wasted potential. They started converting cafés and lounges into long-hour working spaces — WiFi, free coffee, discounted menus. Show up, work, leave. No deposit, no commitment. myHQ was born.
Founded
2016–17 · Proof of Concept
100+ bookings a day. 20+ cafés. Startups were hooked.
Startups and freelancers loved the model — no lock-in, no security deposit, just show up and get to work. Word spread fast. Within months, myHQ was clocking 100+ daily bookings across 20+ cafés in NCR. The idea worked. Now it needed capital to grow.
2017 · Angel Round
Neeraj Singh (Spinny) · Ashneer Grover (BharatPe)
20+ SpacesAngel Round · 2017
2017 · Angel Round
Founder-to-founder belief. First outside capital raised.
Neeraj Singh was building Spinny. Ashneer Grover was about to start BharatPe. They backed myHQ because they recognised the zero-to-one pattern. VCs were still debating whether coworking was a real category. Operators already knew.
2018 · Phase 2 begins
Businesses started asking for dedicated space. Assisted Marketplace was born.
The On Demand product was growing — but businesses using the platform were asking for something more. They needed dedicated, long-term workspace. myHQ built it: Assisted Marketplace, a platform-led advisory for businesses finding office space. Over 100 coworking spaces were onboarded. A new product category was live.
2018 · Pre-Series A
Anupam Mittal
100+ CoworkingsAssisted Marketplace Launched
2019 · Three products taking shape
Virtual Office launched from a Shopify store. Series A followed.
Businesses needed more than a desk — they needed an address for office & GST registration. myHQ launched Virtual Office, initially from a Shopify website. It was scrappy, but it worked. Three products were now live — On Demand, Assisted Marketplace, Virtual Office — and India Quotient backed the platform with a Series A.
2019 · Series A
Led by India Quotient
Series A · India QuotientVirtual Office Born
2020 · COVID
Offices shut overnight. The business went dark. Buyout offers came in. They said no to every one.
COVID didn't ease myHQ into a downturn — it turned the lights off. Overnight. Revenue dropped. The future looked bleak. Multiple acquirers came with buyout offers. The founders turned every single one down. They believed in what they were building. They were waiting for the right partner — not a distressed exit.
Refused every offer
2022 · Phase 3 begins
Powered by ANAROCK. Brand stays. Founders stay. Mission stays.
Recognizing myHQ's strong technology foundation and scalable business model, ANAROCK became a strategic investor. This wasn't a rescue. It was the right fit. ANAROCK understood commercial real estate at the deepest level, and they wanted myHQ to keep operating as myHQ. The brand was retained. The founders stayed on board. The business verticals were now fully live across metro cities, backed by institutional credibility that opened enterprise doors that hadn't been reachable before.
2022 · Powered by ANAROCK
Majority stake · Brand & founders retained
ANAROCK 20223 Verticals · All Metro Cities
2024 · WeWork Partnership & Investment
myHQ becomes WeWork India's exclusive tech, marketing & distribution partner.
WeWork invested in myHQ, making it WeWork India's exclusive partner for On Demand and Virtual Office products. The power of India's largest flex operator combined with myHQ's platform. A partnership that added significant inventory and institutional scale overnight.
WeWork · Exclusive Partner
2025–26 · Going global
Virtual Office in Dubai. Assisted Marketplace in London. Same model. New geographies.
myHQ is now beyond India. Virtual Office launched in Dubai. Assisted Marketplace is expanding into London. The same operator-agnostic, platform-led model that worked in Delhi NCR is now being applied internationally through myhqspaces.com. The ambition is no longer domestic — it's global.
Dubai · Londonmyhqspaces.com
2030 · The target
35X in 3 years. 10X more by 2030. ₹5,000 Cr in transactions.
From ₹20 Cr in FY22 to ₹1,200 Cr in FY26 — that's 35X in three years. The next chapter ends at ₹5,000 Cr in transactions and ₹500 Cr in revenue by FY30. Same platform. Same mission. The market for flexible commercial real estate in India alone is massive — and myHQ has barely scratched the surface of it.

Business Verticals

myHQ operates three distinct business lines under one platform. Each vertical serves a different customer need, runs its own PnL, and has its own leadership. They share infrastructure, brand, and operator relationships — but each is a standalone product with its own market.

To empower the new age workforce with freedom and flexibility to work in any way that they want and from anywhere by providing technology driven innovative workspace solutions.

myHQ offices are present across Bengaluru, Gurugram, New Delhi, Noida, Hyderabad, Chennai, Pune and Ahmedabad. All business verticals share the same platform, operator network, and brand — which means every transaction in one vertical strengthens the others.

Understanding all business verticals provides a complete picture of how myHQ creates value. Our offerings cater to the entire workspace lifecycle for corporates—from Virtual Offices for business registration, to On-Demand workspaces for occasional use, and Assisted Marketplace (AM) for teams seeking a full-fledged, long-term office solution.
